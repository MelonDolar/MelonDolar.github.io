AFL Teams Across Australia

Exploring the Geographic Distribution, Success, and Impact of Australian Football League Teams

Understanding AFL in Australia

The Australian Football League (AFL) is the elite professional competition in Australian rules football. With 18 teams spread across Australia, the league has a rich history dating back over 100 years. This visualisation explores how AFL teams are distributed geographically, their performance over time, and what factors contribute to their success.

Geographic Distribution of AFL Teams

AFL teams are concentrated in certain states, particularly Victoria (home to 10 teams) and Western Australia (2 teams). This map shows the location of each team's home ground.

Number of AFL Teams by State

Victoria dominates the AFL with 10 teams, while most other states have only 1-2 teams. This reflects the sport's origins in Melbourne.

Premiership Wins by State

Victoria teams have won the vast majority of premierships in AFL history. However, Western Australian teams have been competitive in recent decades.

Top 10 Teams by All-Time Wins

Some AFL teams have accumulated significantly more wins than others. Carlton, Melbourne, and Essendon lead in total wins across their histories.

Premiership Wins by Team

Essendon and Carlton lead with 16 premierships each. Richmond and Melbourne are also successful with 13 and 12 premierships respectively.

Average Team Wins Over Recent Seasons (2015-2023)

This chart shows the average number of wins per team season over the past decade. A slight decline in average wins suggests more competitive balance.

When Were AFL Teams Founded?

Most AFL teams were founded in the 1800s. Newer additions (Gold Coast Suns, Greater Western Sydney Giants) joined in the 2010s to expand the competition nationally.

AFL Stadium Capacities

The MCG in Melbourne is the world's largest stadium by capacity at over 100,000 seats. Stadium size influences team revenue and fan experience.

Overall Team Success Ranking (Wins + Premierships)

This ranking combines all-time wins and premiership victories to identify the most successful AFL franchises. Essendon, Carlton, and Collingwood emerge as the top teams.

Key Insights

  • Geographic Concentration: Victoria dominates with 10 of 18 AFL teams, reflecting the sport's origins.
  • Historical Success: Older teams (founded in 1800s) have accumulated more wins and premierships.
  • Competitive Balance: Recent seasons show more competitive balance, with several teams capable of winning premierships.
  • Stadium Influence: Larger stadiums enable more revenue and resources for successful teams.
  • National Expansion: Newer teams from Queensland and New South Wales represent AFL's national growth.
